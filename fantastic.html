Scenario

As the year 2024 came to a close, five friends (Althea, Gabby, Danny, Jenny & Roger) decided to meet up with a shared goal: reviewing their finances before stepping into 2025. Each of them had unique circumstances—credit card bills—and they wanted to better understand their financial health. However, as they began looking through their statements, they felt overwhelmed by the sheer complexity of their transactions and patterns. Realizing they needed help, they reached out to a data analyst who could uncover insights from their financial data, helping them make sense of their spending habits and empowering them with actionable recommendations for the year ahead.

Objective

Define and understand the financial trends of the individuals.

Key Questions

What trends exist over time?

What categories have the most and least spending?

Data Collection

To ensure the integrity of this independent project, I developed a systematic data collection process that can be understood by the participants. The framework of the data collection was quantitative data as I requested the participants last 3 credit card statements to get a better understanding of their shareholders objective and a starting guideline to the overall project. To maintain consistency and accuracy, it was important to have guidelines to input the data into the spreadsheet or first party data in data science terms. Data was organized as followed:

Guidelines

  • Standardized data formats in Excel, SQL & Tableau.
  • Seperated raw & processed Data.
  • Consistent data dictionary.
  • Version control.
  • Data validation for inconsistencies.

    • Limitations

    External Market Influences

    Credit card usage can be influenced by the world/country's economic status, unexpected events and seasonal spending trends. In this project's case the months of the credit card statements can be referred to as "Holidays' which inaccurately reflect their spending.

    Regulatory & Privacy Constraints

    Compliance with PCI-DSS, GDPR, and other financial regulations limits access to certain customer details, restricting the depth of analysis on individual behaviors and trends.

    Analyzation

    Refer to the examples of performing calculations in SQL that illustrated my data analysis process. I have a wide and extensive range of data analysis knowledge. Such as performing simple aggregate functions to Common Table Expressions (CTE's), Subquery's, Window Functions & JOIN.
